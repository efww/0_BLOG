Creating a blueprint or plan for developing your program involves several key steps:

1. **Define Objectives:** Clearly specify what you want your program to accomplish. For example, backtest a trading strategy on Bitcoin data.

2. **Break Down the Tasks:** Divide the overall goal into smaller, manageable components or modules—such as data loading, indicator calculation, strategy logic, backtesting engine, and results analysis.

3. **Design the Workflow:** Map out how these components will interact. Decide the sequence of operations and data flow.

4. **Determine Data Requirements:** Identify what data you need, its format, and how you'll obtain or preprocess it.

5. **Create the Architecture:** Sketch a rough structure or outline of your project, including file organization and key functions or classes.

6. **Set Milestones:** Establish small goals or deliverables for each part, like implementing data loading first, then indicators, etc.

7. **Develop and Test Modules Incrementally:** Build each module step-by-step, testing as you go to ensure correctness.

8. **Integrate and Refine:** Combine modules, troubleshoot issues, and improve performance or robustness.
